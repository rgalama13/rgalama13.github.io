Welcome to Municipality Of Mabinay

Mabinay in the province of Negros Oriental is the perfect destination for the traveler seeking true beauty and stunning nature in the Philippines. Breathtaking views of valleys, brilliant outlooks towards the sunrise over the cliffs and mountains and plenty of caves are all on offer in Mabinay. Travelers looking to experience natural beauty in the Philippines can look no further than the heart of Negros Island, Mabinay.

About the Town

Caves and Nature

The municipality of Mabinay in Negros Oriental, known as the "Cave Capital of the Philippines," is home to over 400 caves. A number of these have been mapped and can be explored, with some being suitable for beginners and others reserved for experienced spelunkers.

Natural Springs

Mabinay Spring Resort, located in the heart of Mabinay, Negros Oriental, is a natural spring resort approximately a two-hour drive from Bacong. This local favorite offers a lagoon with crystal-clear, spring-fed water surrounded by large trees, as well as a separate man-made swimming pool.

Local Cuisine

Pableo's Grill and Restaurant is an affordable Filipino restaurant located on the National Highway in Mabinay, Negros Oriental. Known for its selection of grilled meat and seafood, it features a unique seaman-inspired ambiance and is built from upcycled materials, including shipping container houses.

Festivals & Culture

The Langub Festival is an annual celebration in Mabinay, Negros Oriental, highlighting the town's title as the "Cave Capital of the Philippines". "Langub" is the Cebuano word for "cave".
